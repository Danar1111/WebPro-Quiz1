Kediri

Kediri, located in East Java, Indonesia, boasts a rich history as one of the cultural and historical centers of the region, with its renowned Kediri Kingdom dating back to the 11th century. This city is famous for its traditional Javanese dance, such as the "remo" dance, and traditional art forms like "Kamasan" painting. Kediri's culinary scene offers delights like "sate kelapa" and "soto Kediri," while its textiles and agriculture contribute significantly to the local economy. Notable attractions include the grand Penataran Temple, picturesque beaches like Soge and Tamanan, and a vibrant local culture celebrated through traditional ceremonies and festivals. Kediri stands as a captivating blend of history, culture, and natural beauty in East Java.
